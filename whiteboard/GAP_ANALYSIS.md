# Whiteboard Gap Analysis And Customer Readiness Matrix

Purpose: show how the interactive whiteboard turns expected OT architecture into a customer-safe discovery conversation. The board is not presented as the customer's confirmed architecture. It is a workshop scaffold used to validate what exists, expose unknowns, identify gaps, and scope a Dragos proof of value.

## Presenter Guardrails

- Say: "This is the workshop model I use to validate current state, expected controls, and PoV requirements."
- Do not say: "This is the customer's exact confirmed network."
- If the customer says a control does not exist, treat it as a gap and a PoV/scoping input, not a mistake in the drawing.
- Keep remediation and monitoring distinct: Dragos can prove traffic and visibility quickly; segmentation, MFA, and jump-host remediation stay customer-owned decisions.
- Say "passive SPAN/TAP copy" when discussing sensors so nobody hears "inline control path."
- Say "approved outbound detections/case handoff" when discussing SOC workflow so nobody hears "enterprise tools reach into OT."
- Tie every concern back to a practical close item: SPAN/TAP source, baseline window, systems in scope, SOC workflow, authorization artifacts, and success criteria.
